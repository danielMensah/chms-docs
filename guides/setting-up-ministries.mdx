A ministry in Bethel ChMS is an organisational grouping for teams, departments, or service groups within a branch. Ministries are not events. They help you organise who is responsible for what, whether that's a worship team, an ushering department, or a children's ministry.

<Info>
**Who can do this:** Branch Admin and Editor can create, edit, and manage ministry rosters. Only Branch Admin can delete a ministry. Organisation-level roles have read-only access.
</Info>

## Creating a ministry

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the ministries page">
    In the sidebar, click **Ministries**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new ministry">
    Click **Add Ministry**. A dialog will open with the ministry form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    Enter a **Name** for the ministry. The name must be unique within your branch. Optionally, add a **Description** to explain the ministry's purpose.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the ministry">
    Click **Save**. The ministry is created with an **Active** status by default.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Assigning members

Once a ministry exists, you can start adding members to it and assigning them roles.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the ministry">
    Click on the ministry you want to add members to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a member">
    Click **Add Member**. A dialog will open with a search field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search and select">
    Search for a member by name, then select them from the results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a role">
    Choose a role for this member: **Leader**, **Assistant Leader**, or **Member**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the joined date and confirm">
    Set the **Joined Date** and click **Save** to add them to the roster.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
A member can only be assigned to a ministry within their own branch.
</Info>

## Ministry roles explained

Each person in a ministry is assigned one of three roles:

- **Leader:** The primary person responsible for the ministry. Ministry leaders may have additional portal permissions in future.
- **Assistant Leader:** Supports the ministry leader in coordinating the team.
- **Member:** A general participant in the ministry.

<Tip>
A ministry can have multiple leaders and assistant leaders. For example, a large worship ministry might have two leaders who alternate between services.
</Tip>

## Removing a member from a ministry

When you remove someone from a ministry, Bethel ChMS sets a **Leave Date** on their assignment rather than deleting the record. This preserves their tenure history.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the ministry roster">
    Navigate to the ministry and view its roster.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove the member">
    Find the member you want to remove and click **Remove**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm removal">
    Confirm the action. Bethel ChMS will set a leave date on the assignment, and the member will no longer appear in the active roster.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If a member re-joins a ministry later, a new assignment record is created. Their previous tenure is preserved as a separate record.
</Note>

## Viewing the roster

The ministry detail page shows all active members, those with no leave date. For each person, you can see their name, role, and joined date.

## Archiving a ministry

Archiving a ministry sets its status to **Archived**. You can no longer assign new members to an archived ministry, but the existing roster and history are preserved. You can reactivate an archived ministry at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the ministry">
    Navigate to the ministry you want to archive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive the ministry">
    Click **Archive**. The ministry status changes to **Archived**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Use archiving when a ministry is on pause but you want to keep the history. For example, a seasonal outreach team that runs every December.
</Tip>

## Deleting a ministry

<Warning>
Only Branch Admins can delete a ministry.
</Warning>

Deleting a ministry is a soft delete. Bethel ChMS automatically closes all active assignments and moves the ministry to a deleted state. Deleted ministries can be recovered within 30 days.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the ministry">
    Navigate to the ministry you want to delete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete the ministry">
    Click **Delete** and confirm the action. All active member assignments are closed automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If you just want to pause a ministry temporarily, consider archiving it instead. Archiving keeps the ministry visible in your list and preserves the roster without requiring recovery.
</Note>
