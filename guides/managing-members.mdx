Bethel ChMS tracks two types of people in your church: **members** and **visitors**. Members belong to your congregation. Visitors have attended but have not joined yet.

Use **Members** for your member directory, demographics, household links, group links, portal access, and member QR codes. Use **Visitors** for first-time and returning visitors, follow-up, and conversion to member records.

<Info>
**Who can do this:** Org Owner, Org Admin, Branch Admin, and Editor can create and edit members and visitors in the app. Org Owner, Org Admin, and Branch Admin can delete them. Importing members is available to Org Owner, Org Admin, and Branch Admin. See [roles and permissions](/guides/roles-and-permissions) for your organisation's role model.
</Info>

## Members

### Adding a member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the member list">
    In the sidebar, click **Members** to open the member list for your branch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Member">
    Click the **Add Member** button to open the new member dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the member details">
    Enter the required fields: **First Name** and **Last Name**. You can also fill in optional fields: **Date of Birth**, **Age Group**, **Gender**, **Nationality**, **Email**, **Mobile Number**, **Address**, **Post Code**, and any configured custom fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the member">
    Click **Create Member**. The member is created with a **Newcomer** status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/guides/add-member-dialog.png" alt="Add member dialog showing required and optional fields" />
</Frame>

### Email and mobile uniqueness

When you add or edit a member, Bethel ChMS checks whether the email address or mobile number already belongs to another member in the same branch. If it finds a match, the form shows a warning with a link to the existing record.

<Tip>
Use the warning to review the existing record before you save. For higher-confidence duplicate pairs, use the **Possible Duplicates** queue and merge workflow.
</Tip>

### Age groups on member records

Age groups help you filter the member directory and report on demographics.

- If you enter **Date of Birth**, Bethel ChMS derives the member's age group from your organisation's age-group configuration.
- If you leave **Date of Birth** blank, you can choose **Age Group** manually.
- If both are present, **Date of Birth** takes precedence.

Use [age groups](/guides/age-groups) to configure the organisation-wide age brackets.

### Editing a member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the member's profile">
    Navigate to the member list and click on the member you want to edit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Edit">
    On the member detail page, click **Edit** to open the editing sheet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update fields and save">
    Make your changes and click **Save Changes**. If you change the email or mobile number, duplicate detection runs again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The member detail page also shows attendance history, ministry assignments, household information, group information, custom fields, and change history where available.

### Deleting a member

<Warning>
Editors cannot delete members.
</Warning>

Deleting a member is a soft delete. The record is hidden but not permanently removed immediately. Attendance history remains available for historical reporting.

<Note>
When a member is deleted, active ministry assignments are closed.
</Note>

### Lifecycle statuses

Every member in Bethel ChMS has one of three lifecycle statuses:

- **Newcomer**, assigned automatically when a member is first created.
- **Active**, indicates the member is regularly attending.
- **Inactive**, indicates the member has stopped attending.

You can manually change a member's status from the member edit form.

<Accordion title="How automation rules work">
Bethel ChMS can automatically update lifecycle statuses based on attendance patterns. Here's how the defaults work:

- **Newcomer to Active:** A member is promoted to Active after 4 attendances within 90 days.
- **Active to Inactive:** A member is moved to Inactive after 30 days of absence.

These thresholds are configured at the organisation level, with optional per-branch overrides. Automation is disabled by default. To enable it, an Org Owner can go to **Organisation** > **Automation**. See [Automation rules](/guides/automation-rules).
</Accordion>

### Filtering and searching

Use the search bar at the top of the member list to find members by first name, last name, email, or mobile number.

You can filter or sort by:

- **Status**
- **Nationality**
- **Age Group**
- **Gender**
- Active member custom fields

Click **Clear all** to remove active filters. Click **Export CSV** to export the filtered list.

### Merging duplicate members

Bethel ChMS can surface possible duplicate member pairs. When duplicates exist for the selected branch, the member list shows a banner with **Review pairs**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open possible duplicates">
    From **Members**, click **Review pairs** in the duplicate banner. You can also open `/organisation/members/duplicates` directly in the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the pair">
    Compare the two records. Click **Not a duplicate** to dismiss the pair, or click **Merge members** to start the merge wizard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the survivor record">
    Choose which record to keep. For fields such as **Email**, **Mobile**, **Address**, **Date of birth**, and **Nationality**, choose whether to keep the survivor value or adopt the other record's value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and confirm">
    Review the shared-attendance warning, type `MERGE`, and click **Merge permanently**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Merging members is permanent. If both records attended the same event, duplicate attendance rows for the discarded record are permanently deleted.
</Warning>

You can also select exactly two same-branch members in the member list and click **Merge**.

## Visitors

### What is a visitor?

A **visitor** is someone who has attended your church but is not yet a member. Visitors are tracked separately so your team can follow up with them and, when the time is right, convert them into full members.

### Adding a visitor

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the visitor list">
    In the sidebar, click **Visitors** to open the visitor list for your branch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Visitor">
    Click the **Add Visitor** button to open the new visitor dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in visitor details">
    Enter the required fields: **First Name** and **Last Name**. You can also fill in optional fields: **Gender**, **Nationality**, **Email**, **Mobile Number**, and any configured visitor custom fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the visitor">
    Click **Create Visitor**. The visit count is set to 1, and the first and last visit dates are recorded automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/guides/add-visitor-dialog.png" alt="Add visitor dialog" />
</Frame>

### Visit tracking

Bethel ChMS automatically tracks three fields for every visitor:

- **Visit count**, increments each time the visitor checks in to an event.
- **Last visit date**, updates to the most recent check-in date.
- **First visit date**, set when the visitor is created and never changes.

These fields are system-managed. They update when the visitor is checked in through attendance or public check-in flows.

### Finding visitors to follow up

The visitor page includes stat cards for total visitors, new visitors this month, returning visitors, and visitors who are ready to convert. Click the **Ready to Convert** card to filter the list to visitors with at least 2 visits.

Use the search bar to find visitors by name, email, or mobile number. You can also filter by **Nationality** and visitor custom fields. Click **Export CSV** to export the filtered list.

### Converting a visitor to a member

When a visitor is ready to join your church as a member, you can convert their record directly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the visitor's record">
    In the visitor list, find the visitor you want to convert.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Convert to Member">
    In the visitor row, click **Convert**. A dialog opens with the visitor's details pre-filled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and add details">
    Review the pre-filled fields and make any corrections. You can also add **Date of Birth**, **Address**, and **Post Code**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the conversion">
    Click **Convert to Member**. Bethel ChMS creates a new member record with **Newcomer** status and soft-deletes the visitor record.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
The new member stores a link to the original visitor record through the conversion reference. On the member's dedicated profile page, the **Attendance** tab includes attendance recorded before conversion as visitor attendance, alongside attendance recorded after conversion as member attendance.
</Info>

<Tip>
If the visitor's email or mobile number already belongs to an existing member in the same branch, you'll see a warning before the conversion completes. Check the existing member record to avoid creating a duplicate.
</Tip>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/guides/convert-visitor-to-member.png" alt="Convert visitor to member dialog with pre-filled fields" />
</Frame>

### Deleting a visitor

<Warning>
Editors cannot delete visitors.
</Warning>

Deleting a visitor is a soft delete. The visitor is hidden from active lists.
